Locatour exists to do one thing: **help people create memorable experiences out
in the world.**

That sentence is our whole compass. Every feature in the app is judged against
it. If something makes the game more fun on the couch but less likely to get you
somewhere worth remembering, we don't build it.

## Why a game?

Getting outdoors is good for us, and we all know it, yet the day slips by and we
never go. A great game is one of the most reliable ways to turn "I should" into
"I did." Locatour borrows the gentle pull of levelling up, streaks, and
discovery, and points it at something genuinely worthwhile: standing in a real
place, looking at a real view, making a real memory.

The points are a means, not the end. Nobody remembers the experience bar filling
up. They remember the lookout at golden hour, the waterfall they'd never have
found, the morning their kid asked to "go check in somewhere new."

## Public places, owned by everyone

Locatour is deliberately built around **public, non-commercial land:**
state, national, and council-owned parks, reserves, beaches, lookouts, trails,
and monuments. These places belong to all of us, they're free to enjoy, and
they're often overlooked. Sending people to them is a civic act: it builds
connection to your neighbourhood, your city, and your state.

We don't send you to businesses, and we don't sell your attention to them.

## Explore gently: conservation by design

Some of the most special places are also the most fragile: a quiet waterfall, a
small reserve with no car park, a stretch of coast that a crowd would ruin.
Locatour is designed so those places aren't blasted to everyone at once. They
open up gradually, to explorers who've shown they're invested, which keeps the
visitor numbers gentle and the experience special. The game's progression
doubles as crowd control and care for the land. You can read how that works in
[Hidden locations](/play/hidden-locations/).

## What we're really building

A reason to go. A nudge toward the door. A scoreboard for a life spent a little
more outside, and a quiet way to look after the places that make it worth the
trip.

That's Locatour. Now go make a memory.
