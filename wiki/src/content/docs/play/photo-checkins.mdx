A **check-in** is how you prove you made it. It's the single action at the
centre of the whole game: you're at a place, you check in, you earn experience.

## How a check-in works

1. **You arrive at the place.** Check-ins are proximity-based: you have to
   actually be there.
2. **Your phone confirms you're there.** Locatour uses your device's location to
   check that you're at the spot, not somewhere else.
3. **You take a photo.** You snap a quick photo, live, in the app: a little
   memento of the moment and your visit.
4. **You're checked in.** Experience is awarded, your streak ticks over, and the
   moment is saved to your history.

## Why a photo?

Three reasons, in order of importance:

- **It's the memory.** The photo is the souvenir, the thing you'll scroll back
  through later. Locatour is about [creating memorable
  experiences](/start/memorable-experiences/), and the photo is how each one
  gets saved.
- **It marks that you were present.** Taking a photo on the spot is part of being
  *there*, not just passing through.
- **It keeps the game fair.** Real check-ins from real visits are what make
  levels and achievements mean something.

## Share a check-in

Made it somewhere special? You can share any of your check-ins as a link. Open a
check-in from your profile, tap **Share**, and Locatour creates a public page for
that moment (your photo, the place, and when you were there) that you can send
to anyone.

It's the easy way to show a friend the hidden waterfall you found, or to look
back on a favourite day out. Only the check-ins you choose to share get a link,
and anyone you send it to can open it without a Locatour account, so share the
moments you're happy to make public.

## Keeping it fair

Locatour is a game of genuine visits, so we take fairness seriously. The app is
designed to recognise a real, in-person check-in and to gently discourage ways
of faking one. We keep the exact details of those checks under the hood, partly
so they keep working, and partly because honest players never need to think
about them. If you actually went, you're fine.

<Aside type="note" title="If a check-in doesn't work">
- **"You're not close enough."** Get a bit nearer to the exact spot and try again.
- **"We can't pin down your location."** Step into the open, away from tall
  buildings or dense tree cover, and retry: your phone needs a clear view of the sky.
- **Location is turned off.** Make sure location services and Locatour's location
  permission are enabled.
- **You're offline.** No signal? No problem: Locatour can save your check-in and
  finish it when you're back online.
</Aside>
